About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate at the University at Buffalo specializing in autonomous systems, SLAM, and risk-aware navigation for robots in complex terrains. My research develops algorithms for robust perception and navigation in challenging environments.

Research Focus

Enabling legged robots to navigate unstructured environments through:

Terrain-adaptive locomotion Visual perception Dynamic motion planning Risk-aware navigation

I work in the Distributed Robotics and Networked Embedded Sensing (DRONES) Lab under Prof. Karthik Dantu, creating robotic systems that operate autonomously in real-world scenarios.

My approach combines theoretical foundations with practical implementations, focusing on developing algorithms that enable robots to perceive, reason, and act in dynamic environments. I'm particularly interested in bridging the gap between simulation and real-world deployment.

Prior to my Ph.D., I developed machine learning solutions for embedded systems and worked on autonomous navigation for agricultural robots. I'm passionate about creating technologies that push the boundaries of what autonomous systems can achieve.
